{{_('About the tiger mosquito and yellow fever')}}

{{_('The tiger mosquito (Aedes albopictus) and the yellow fever mosquito (Aedes aegypti) are invasive mosquitoes and vectors for diseases. They have several features in common: they are small, dark, and with lines and white spots over their bodies and their legs. They live mostly in urban areas, where they breed in small containers with some stagnant water.')}}

{{_('How do we distinguish them? Easily! The tiger mosquito is black (very black!) and has one single white line in the head and thorax (the back). However, the yellow fever mosquito is dark brown (very dark!) and has 4 white lines in the shape of a lira in its thorax. Check the thorax to recognize them better!')}}

{{_('About the diseases')}}

{{_('These mosquitoes are vectors of the arbovirus worldwide like denge, yellow fever, chikungunya, and Zika. The arbovirus are a group of viruses that are transmitted via arthropods that act like vectors. These diseases can prodcues fever and muscle and joint pain, among other symptoms and in some cases the patient needs to be hospitalized. The virus is transmitted when a mosquito from the Aedes genus bites a person after being infected from a previous bit from another infected person.')}}

{{_('Knowing and understanding the distribution of the mosquitoes and its presence of these two vectors is very important to prevent the spread of diseases and control the different plans of intervention. These diseases have increased in the last 50 years, as these two species have moved to new areas.')}}
